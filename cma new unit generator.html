📖 Glossary of Banking & CMA Terms

Deemed Export
Goods supplied within India but treated as exports under Govt. policy — eligible for export benefits without physical export. Examples: Supplies to SEZ, EOU, EPCG holders, Advance Authorisation holders, World Bank/ADB funded projects, Defence establishments. Payment received in Indian Rupees (not foreign currency) but supplier gets duty drawback, GST refund, and advance authorisation benefits.
Usance Period (LC context)
Credit period allowed by overseas supplier to Indian importer — time gap between shipment date and actual payment date. A "90-day Usance LC" means payment due 90 days after bill of lading. Also called "Time LC" or "Term LC". Sight LC = payment immediately on document presentation (zero usance). Longer usance = larger FLC limit required by bank.
Inland LC (ILC)
Guarantee by borrower's bank to domestic supplier that payment will be made on due date. Used for indigenous raw material purchases on credit. Limit = (Annual Indigenous Purchases ÷ 365) × Credit Period × (1 − Margin%).
MPBF — Maximum Permissible Bank Finance
Maximum Working Capital finance a bank can provide. Per Tandon Committee: Method I = 75% of CA minus other CL. Method II = 75% of CA minus all CL (requires 25% borrower margin). MPBF = recommended CC/OD limit.
DSCR — Debt Service Coverage Ratio
(PAT + Depreciation + TL Interest) ÷ (Principal Repayment + TL Interest). Measures ability to repay term loan. Bank norm: Average DSCR ≥ 1.5 to 2.0. Below 1.0 = unit cannot repay from own cash flows.
IRR — Internal Rate of Return
Discount rate at which NPV of all project cash flows = zero. Represents expected annual return on project investment. If IRR > Cost of Capital → project is financially viable. Used in project appraisal for term loans.
Break-Even Point (BEP)
Sales level where Total Revenue = Total Cost (zero profit/loss). BEP (₹) = Fixed Costs ÷ P/V Ratio. Margin of Safety = Actual Sales − BEP. For sound appraisal, BEP should be below 60–70% of projected sales capacity.
Bank Guarantee (BG)
Bank commits to pay a third party (govt dept / client / tender authority) if borrower defaults on contractual obligation. Types: Performance BG, Financial BG, Bid Bond/EMD. Counter guarantee = security given by borrower to bank (typically 100% in FDs or property).
WDV vs SLM Depreciation
WDV: Fixed % on opening book value each year — amount reduces as book value reduces. Prescribed by Income Tax Act. SLM: Fixed % on original cost every year — constant amount throughout asset life. Prescribed by Companies Act 2013.
