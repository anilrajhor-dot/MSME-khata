Depreciation Settings AUTO-CALCULATED

Depreciation is calculated as Rate % × Opening Balance of each fixed-asset class (Opening = previous year's closing value; for the first year column, the entered value is used as opening). Both WDV and SLM are applied as a rate on the opening balance — for SLM, set the rate as Original Cost ÷ Useful Life, expressed as a % of current balance.
For projected years, depreciation auto-reduces each asset class's closing balance (after applying any growth/capex % from the table below), and the resulting depreciation amount flows into the Profit & Loss Account and Balance Sheet automatically.

Fixed Assets, Capital & Long-Term Items % GROWTH

CMA Form V — Working Capital Assessment 4 METHODS

RBI-prescribed methods. Turnover Method for limits ≤ ₹5 Crore. MPBF (Tandon) for limits > ₹5 Crore. Cash Budget for seasonal industries.
Which method to use:
📌 Turnover Method — Limit ≤ ₹5 Crore (₹500 lakh). Formula: 20% of projected annual sales.
📌 MPBF Method I — Limit > ₹5 Crore. Formula: Min(75% CA, WCG where WCG = CA − Other CL).
📌 MPBF Method II — Alternative. Formula: 75% CA − All CL (excl. CC). Higher margin from borrower.
📌 Cash Budget Method — Seasonal/project-based. Peak month cash deficit = WC limit.

User Manual HELP

1. Getting Started

Go to Setup first. Enter the unit's name, constitution, activity type and bank details. Set the number of years (3–5) and the base year, then click Generate Year Columns. Each column represents one financial year — typically 2 audited years, 1 estimated (current) year, and 1–2 projected years. You can rename any column label directly.

2. Trial Balance

Enter year-wise figures for each line item under Assets, Liabilities, Income and Expenses. Use + Asset/Liability/Income/Expense Line to add custom rows; use the x button to remove a row. The app recalculates automatically on every change. The Trial Balance panel shows a tally check — if Total Assets ≠ Total Liabilities for any year, a warning is shown with the exact difference, usually traceable to Reserves & Surplus or Capital entries.

3. P&L / Balance Sheet

Auto-generated Trading & Manufacturing Account, Profit & Loss Account, and Balance Sheet, derived entirely from the Trial Balance entries.

4. CMA Forms I–V

  • CMA-I (Operating Statement): Sales, COGS, Gross/Operating/Net Profit, and Net Cash Accrual.
  • CMA-II (Analysis of Balance Sheet): Net Worth, Long-Term Sources, Current Liabilities, Current Assets, Net Working Capital, Current Ratio, Debt/Equity.
  • CMA-III (Comparative CA/CL): Item-wise current assets and liabilities with year-on-year NWC movement.
  • CMA-IV (Fund Flow): Sources and Uses of Funds between consecutive years, with a net-change check that should be close to zero.
  • CMA-V (MPBF): Working Capital Assessment under Tandon Committee Method I (75% of CA less other CL) and Method II (75% of CA less core margin), with recommended limit.

5. Ratios & DSCR

Standard ratios (Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Debt-Equity, TOL/TNW, margins, turnover ratios, Debtors/Creditors Velocity, Break-even Point) are computed automatically.

Break-even Point is shown in two ways: (a) as a Sales Value (₹) — the level of sales at which profit is zero, computed as Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin Ratio; and (b) implicitly, the Margin of Safety = Actual Sales − BEP Sales. Fixed Costs = Admin Expenses + Depreciation + Interest. Variable Costs = COGS. If Contribution Margin is zero or negative, BEP shows as "N/A" (loss at all sales levels).

DSCR: enter Term Loan Interest and Principal repayment obligations for each year. The app computes Cash Accrual (PAT+Depreciation+TL Interest) ÷ Obligation. Bank norms typically expect average DSCR ≥ 1.5–2.0.

6. Drawing Power

Select a year column, then set margin % for each stock/debtor category (defaults to 25%). The app computes Drawing Power = Σ(Value × (1−Margin%)) − Sundry Creditors, and compares it to outstanding CC balance to flag excess drawing.

7. Loan / EMI Schedule

Enter Loan Amount, Annual Interest Rate, Tenure (months), Moratorium period, Disbursement month, and how the moratorium is treated (interest-only, or fully capitalized). Click Calculate EMI & Schedule. The app generates a complete month-by-month table showing Opening Balance, Interest, Principal, EMI paid, and Closing Balance for every calendar month from disbursement to final repayment.

EMI formula used: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n ÷ [(1+r)^n − 1], where P = principal (after any capitalized moratorium interest), r = monthly interest rate, n = remaining tenure in months after moratorium.

8. IRR / NPV

Project IRR/NPV: Set the Discount Rate and Initial Investment (Year 0 outflow). Cash flows for Year 1 onward = PAT + Depreciation for each year column (from CMA-I). NPV = Σ CFₜ ÷ (1+r)^t. IRR is the discount rate at which NPV = 0, found by iterative search (bisection).

Loan IRR: Computed from the Loan/EMI schedule. Year 0 = +Loan Amount (cash inflow to the borrower). Each subsequent year = −(total Principal + Interest paid that year). The resulting IRR is the effective annual cost of borrowing, useful for comparing against the project's return.

9. Data, Save & Export

All data is stored locally on this device (browser localStorage) — nothing is sent anywhere. Use Export JSON regularly to back up your data or move it to another device, and Import JSON to restore it. Reset All Data permanently erases everything on this device — export first if unsure.

10. Troubleshooting

  • Balance Sheet doesn't tally: check Reserves & Surplus / Capital figures against actual TB; the displayed difference tells you the amount to investigate.
  • A red error box appears: note the message — it usually points to the exact calculation step. Try Reset (after exporting a backup) if the data file may be corrupted.
  • App opens but tabs don't respond: try opening the file in Chrome; some browsers restrict local-file scripts.
